History of Unix

Unix began its journey half a century ago, in 1964. A new innovative operating system, called MULTICS, was being created by MIT, AT&T Bell Labs and General Electrics which despite having many problems it proposed different ideas that were new to the world of computer science.

Bell Labs disliking the outcome of the project abandoned it, but a programmer named Ken Thompson, who worked in Bell Labs chose to continue with the project. In 1969, a group of computer scientists led by Thompson and Ritchie implemented a lot of innovative features to the system and despite modifying it to a smaller version, their version was the beginning of Unix.

In 1970, Unix was officially named, and its growth began. A year later, in need of more users, Unix commands were published. In 1973, it was rewritten in programming language C giving to the users a lot more tools.
